Top Gun: Maverick (2022)

Genre: Action, Drama | Runtime: 130 min | Director: Joseph Kosinski

After more than thirty years of service as one of the Navy's top aviators, Pete "Maverick" Mitchell (Tom Cruise) is back where he belongs, pushing the envelope as a courageous test pilot. When he finds himself training a detachment of Top Gun graduates for a specialized mission, Maverick must confront the ghosts of his past and his deepest fears. The mission will require the ultimate sacrifice from those chosen to fly it, and Maverick must prove that he's still the best of the best while grappling with the legacy of his late friend Goose, whose son, Lieutenant Bradley "Rooster" Bradshaw (Miles Teller), is among the pilots he's training.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Joseph Kosinski and Tom Cruise deliver a masterclass in how to make a legacy sequel that honors the original while surpassing it in nearly every way. Top Gun: Maverick is more than just a nostalgic trip—it's a cinematic event that redefines what's possible in aerial action filmmaking.

The film's greatest achievement lies in its revolutionary approach to practical filmmaking. Unlike most modern action movies that rely heavily on CGI, Maverick features real actors flying real fighter jets, captured with innovative camera systems that put audiences directly in the cockpit. The result is some of the most visceral, breathtaking aerial sequences ever committed to film.

Tom Cruise proves why he remains one of Hollywood's last true movie stars, delivering a performance that balances Maverick's signature cockiness with newfound vulnerability and depth. His character arc—from reckless maverick to reluctant mentor—feels earned and emotionally resonant, especially in his interactions with Miles Teller's Rooster.

The supporting cast is exceptional, with Miles Teller perfectly capturing the spirit of Goose's son while establishing his own identity. Jennifer Connelly brings warmth and maturity as Maverick's love interest, and Jon Hamm is perfectly cast as the no-nonsense admiral who represents the changing times.

What makes Maverick truly special is how it balances spectacular action with genuine emotional stakes. The final mission sequence is a white-knuckle thrill ride that had audiences cheering in theaters worldwide, but it's the character relationships that give the action meaning. The film's themes of legacy, mentorship, and overcoming grief resonate deeply.

Final verdict: A perfect blockbuster that delivers everything you want from a summer movie and more. Spectacular action, emotional depth, and a career-defining performance from Tom Cruise make this one of the best legacy sequels ever made.
